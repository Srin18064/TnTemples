General Information


One among most powerful temples of Sri Hayagreevar, located around 3 kilometers from Singaperumal Koil in Chettipunyam is the Yoga Hayagreeva temple. Originally the temple was constructed for Lord Varadaraja Perumal with Goddess Hemambhuja Nayagi. Later on the Urchavar vigraham of Sri Yoga Hayagreevar and Sri Devanatha Swamy were brought from Tiruvahindrapuram. Worshiping Sri Hayagreevar brings wisdom and knowledge to the seekers.

Special Features

Lord Hayagreevar's utsava moorthy is also seen in the main sannadhi in a yogic pose with folded legs and students pray here during examinations with pencils, hall tickets and books to get good results. The idols of Devanadha perumal and Yoga Hayagreeva are believed to have been brought from Thiruvendhipuram divyadesam to protect them from invasions several years back. The utsavar Devanadha perumal has a Prayoga chakra in his right hand.

History

Chettipunyam derived its name because of a community, "Chettiyars", who lived here and earned their living through weaving. Originally the presiding deity of this temple was Lord Sri Varadaraja Perumal along with Goddess Hemambhuja Nayagi. This temple is over 400 years old. It was in 1848 that the idols of Sri Hayagreevar and Sri Devanatha Swamy, which were worshipped in underground chambers at Sri Thiruvahindrapuram, one of the 108 holy places near Cuddalore, were brought by Sri Dewan Rangachar to this village to be safeguarded during alien invasion. Sri Vedanta Desikar had composed a sloka with 32 verses in praise of Lord Sri Hayagrevar, which is popularly known as Sri Hayagreva Stotram. It is believed that worshiping Lord Sri Hayagreva brings wisdom and knowledge to the seekers.
