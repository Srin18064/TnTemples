General Information


This is the 109th Thevara Paadal Petra Shiva Sthalam and 55th Shiva sthalam on the north side of river Kaveri in Chozha Nadu. The place was once called as Pazhuvur and now the temple is in Keezhapalur.

Wosrshipped by


It is believed that Ma Parvati, Brahma, Maha Vishnu, Indra, Agathiyar, Chandran, Suryan, Vasishtar, Kasibar and Vyasar worshiped Lord Shiva of this temple.

Greateness of the Temple

The Sage Parasurama did penance in this place and got rid of his sins of having killed his Mother.

Special Features

Lord Shiva in the temple is a swayambaramurthy.

History

Since Thirugnanasambandar has sung hymns in praise of Lord Shiva of this temple, the original temple might have existed before 7th Century. Latter during Parantaka-I period the temple was reconstructed in to Stone temple and extended further during Vijayanagara period. Another Shiva Temple in the same Village called Pasupatheeswaram ( old name Maravaneeswaram ) was built by King Pazhuvettarayar’s son Kandan also gifted to this temple.
This temple also has the inscriptions of “Perumpazhuvur Pazhuvettarayar”. As per the inscriptions this place was called as Brahmadeya Pazhuvur Alandurai, Kundra Kootram Uththunga Valanattu Sirupazhuvur and Lord Shiva was called as Alandurai Mahadevar. The inscriptions belongs to Kulothunga Chozha, Rajathirajan, Rajendra Chozha and Vijayanagara Dynasty.
There were 23 inscriptions recorded from this temple and most of the inscriptions belongs to Chozha period. The inscriptions mainly talks about the endowment of Naivedyam, burning of lamps, Sacred bath to Lord Shiva and prayers etc,. For the same donations were made in the form of Land from which 2 nazhi rice was received, 50 kasu for Thirumanjanam, 2 kalanju for burning a perpetual lamp, 3.5 ma land to give 2 nazhi rice for Naivedyam and 90 Sheeps for burning one lamp. The inscriptions also records the political informations. The endowments are established by both Male and female.
Rajaraja-I’s 10th reign year inscription starts with his full meikeerthi/ title, records the endowment of naivedyam for which 2 nazhi rice to be supplied from the gifted land. 8th reign year inscription records the endowment of Naivedyam and thirumanjanam, on Chithirai Vishu day, Uttarayanam and Dakshinayanam for which paddy and 50 kasu was gifted to this temple. This gift was given by Perum Pazhuvur Adigal Pazhuvettarayar’s wife Veeranan otriyur.
Rajaraja-I’s 10th reign year inscription records the endowment of 2 nazhi Thumbaipalli thamam Parichattam ( ?), by Adikal Pazhuvettaraiyar Kandan maravanar Perunthirathu Arayan Sundara Chozhan for which a land was gifted to this temple.
Another Kovi Rajakesari’s 8th reign year inscription records the endowment of burning a perpetual lamp for which 90 saava moova peradu / Sheeps Kizhavan Nambanai adikal Pazhuvettaraiyar kandan Maravanar. The same person Kizhavan Nambanai adikal Pazhuvettaraiyar kandan Maravanar’s daughter and his wife gifted 190 kalanju Silver Kalasam.
Vikrama Chozha’s 6th reign year inscription records the endowment of Naivedyam, Kariyamuthu, neiyamuthu, adaikayamuthu and burning of a sandhi lamp for which land measuring 3.25 veli situated in three different places for which the periphery limit are also mentioned. In the inscription it is mentioned the god’s name as Vanavichathiranallur Mudikonda Chozheswaramudayar. Whether this donation is for which temple or not is not known.
Koparakesari’s 13th reign year inscription records the gift of a Cheppu pot by Pampuni Kootrathu Pampuni merchant Kunavan Vadavayilan. ( measuring 70 Cheppu kalaipanai in weight Cheruvidai ).
A loose stone inscription dated Parithabi year Avani month, 22nd day records the endowment of burning a day lamp for which a Thoppu ( farm ) at Elankuruchi by Arasunilaiyitta ( may be an official ) Rangappa Mazhavaraya Pandithar to god of Yogavaneswara Swami.
During Vijayanagara Period 30 pon ( Gold?) tax was exempted in three Villages and gave it to this temple. A person called Ravi Maniyan, constructed a mandapa. Maha Kumbhabhishekam was conducted after thirupani in 1974. Ref : Hand book issued during the Heritage walk.
