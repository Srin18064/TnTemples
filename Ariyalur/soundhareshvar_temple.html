History

This temple was arosen built by Sundhara Chola in A.D 962. According to local traditions, it was Rati who did penance and got back her husband Manmad, hence the name of the village Kama-rati-valli. To the east of this village is situated a village called Asayamanavalam. It is named after Rathi's beautiful husband, i.e. Ajjayamanavalan (The Beautiful Husband-Cupid.) A beautiful bronze image of Rathi is also preserved in this temple.
Another story preserved in this temple is that the snake king Karkotaka got rid of his curse by worshiping Lord Shiva in this village and hence the Ishwar of the temple is called Karkotaka Ishwar. A slab with figures depicting this temple is also preserved in this temple. The temple has more than 40 inscriptions from the Chola, Pandya and Hoysala periods. Inscriptions refer to the temple name as Tirunallur Srikovil Mahadeva, Thirunallurparameswarar and Tiruparkotaka Easvaratu Mahadeva. Inscriptions refer to this village as a Brahmadeya in Mirai Khurram, Kamadavallichathurvedi Mangalam. The temple has beautiful sculptures and bronzes of the Chola period.
